About Me

My name is Will Boulton and I am a software engineer and mathematician. I finished my BA and MMath at the University of Cambridge in 2016. Since then I have been working as a software engineer, as well as doing mathematical modelling and data science type things.

This website is a huge mish-mash of topics I am interested in as well as a place for my to collect together various articles I am reading, or want to read. Occasionally there might be some useful information for my maths students. I will put stuff on this website that I'm excited to find out more about, and mainly hope that this website can be used to help other students in a similar situation.
numpy matplotlib seaborn
The sort of areas I am interested in include:

  • molecular biology, genomics, biochemistry, and molecular dynamics
  • applied mathematics, particularly in life science
  • bioinformatics and computational biology
  • machine learning and data science
  • applied category theory, and category theory in computer science
  • coalgebra
  • recreational mathematics
  • programming, mainly in Python
  • computer science
Each of these are massive areas!

As a software engineer I mainly program in Python and Java, though I have occasionally played around with Javascript, C and C++. I have some interest in Haskell, due to its connection to category theory, which I've remained interested in since university.
